"Painter" redirects here. For other uses, see Painter (disambiguation). Color, made up of hue, saturation, and value, dispersed over a surface is the essence of painting, just as pitch and rhythm are the essence of music.
Color is highly subjective, but has observable psychological effects, although these can differ from one culture to the next.
Black is associated with mourning in the West, but in the East, white is.
Some painters, theoreticians, writers and scientists, including Goethe,[3] Kandinsky,[4] and Newton,[5] have written their own color theory.
